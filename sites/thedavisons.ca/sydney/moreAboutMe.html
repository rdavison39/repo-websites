Background

I started playing hockey when I was 6 years old playing house league Tyke and then rep Novice and Atom. At the same time I was heavily invovled in gymnastics and was training 20 hours a week. Needless to say i was very busy between school, hockey and gymnastics. In 2009 I decided I wanted to make the national gymnastics team but this required 31 hours of training per week. It was clear i couldn't do both not because I didn't want to, but because I coudn't physically be in 2 places at once and with hockey being a team sport I couldn't let the team down. So instead I opted to play boys house league hockey where the level of play was challenging but the time commitment was much less and  because of that I was able focus on gymnastics and the hours it required. In 2 years I competed at the national level and came 2nd on bars and 6th overall at the Canadian National Championships held in PEI. I was a huge accomplishment.

 



In 2011 I decided i missed hockey and the fun girls have in the dressing room and rejoined the competitive girls hockey and have never looked back.  I'm telling you about the gymnastics part of my life because I know what hard work means, what dedication means and that when I put my mind to something I can achieve it. My mind is now focused entirely on hockey and I want to be the best hockey offensively and defensively that I can be.

I have so many good memories of hockey, the shoot-outs, our victories and our defeats but mostly my memories are of the friends that I have made. Through hockey I want to continue these experience and continue meeting new people as I go through my post secondary education.
